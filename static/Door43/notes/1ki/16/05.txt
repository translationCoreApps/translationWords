===== translationNotes: =====

  * **are they not written in the book of the events of the kings of Israel?** - This question is used to either inform or remind the readers that the information about Baasha is in this other book. This rhetorical question can be translated as a statement. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:1ki:15:31|15:31]]. AT: "they are written in the book of the events of the kings of Israel." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **are they not written in the book of the events of the kings of Israel?** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "someone has written  about them in the book of the events of the kings of Israel." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **Baasha slept with his ancestors** - Sleeping is a euphemism that represents dying. AT: "Baasha died as his ancestors had" or "Like his ancestors, Baasha died"  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_euphemism]])
  * **and was buried** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "and people buried him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **Tirzah** - This is the name of a city. See how you translated it in [[:en:bible:notes:1ki:14:17|14:17]].
  * **became king in his place** - The phrase "in his place" is a metaphor meaning "instead of him." AT: "became king instead of Baasha" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
