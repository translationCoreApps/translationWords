===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Woe to the land of the rustling of wings, which is along the rivers of Ethiopia** - Possible meanings of "the rustling of wings" are 1) wings represent boats that have sails. AT: "Woe to those who live in the land beyond the rivers of Ethiopia, whose many ships look like insects on the water" or 2) the rustling of wings refers to the noise of insects that have wings, perhaps locusts. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **by the sea** - The Nile River was very wide, and people in Egypt and Ethiopia referred to it as "the sea." AT: "on the great river" or "along the Nile River" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **vessels of papyrus** - Papyrus is a tall plant that grows along the side of the Nile River. People bound together papyrus bundles to make boats. AT: "papyrus boats" or "boats made of reeds" or (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_unknown]])
  * **a nation tall and smooth ... a people feared far and near ... a nation strong and trampling down, whose land the rivers divide** - These phrases all describe the people of one nation.
  * **a nation tall and smooth** - The word "nation" here refers to the people of that nation. AT: "a nation whose people are tall and have smooth skin" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **a people feared far and near** - The words "far" and "near" are used together to mean "everywhere." AT: "a people who are feared everywhere" or "a people that everyone in the earth fears" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_merism]])
  * **a nation strong and trampling down** - Trampling down represents conquering other nations. AT: "a nation that is strong and conquers other nations" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the rivers divide** - This probably refers to many rivers that flow through the nation so they divide it into different parts.
