===== translationNotes: =====

  * **are they not written in the book of the events of the kings of Judah?** - This question is used to either inform or remind the readers that the information about Abijah is in this other book. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:1ki:14:29|14:29]]. This rhetorical question can be translated as a statement. AT: "they are written in the book of the events of the kings of Judah." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **are they not written in the book of the events of the kings of Judah?** - This can stated in active form. AT: "someone has written about this in the book of the events of the kings of Judah." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **between Abijah and Jeroboam** - The names "Abijah" and "Jeroboam" represent the armies of those two kings.  AT: "between the armies of Abijah and Jeroboam" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Abijah slept with his ancestors** - In this euphemism, sleeping represents dying. AT: "Abijah died as his ancestors had" or "Like his ancestors, Abaijah died" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_euphemism]])
  * **became king in his place** - The phrase "in his place" is a metaphor meaning "instead of him." AT: "became king instead of Abijah" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
