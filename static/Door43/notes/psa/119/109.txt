===== translationNotes: =====

  * **My life is always in my hand** - This is an idiom that means the writer's life is always in danger. AT: "My enemies are often trying to kill me" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **I do not forget your law** - This can be stated in positive form. AT: "I always remember your laws" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_litotes]])
  * **The wicked have set a snare for me** - Wicked people trying to capture and kill the writer is spoken of as if they were hunters setting a trap to catch an animal. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **The wicked** - This nominal adjective can be stated as an adjective. AT: "Wicked people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_nominaladj]])
  * **I have not strayed from your instructions** - Not disobeying God's instructions is spoken of as if the writer has not walked away from God's instructions. This can be stated in positive form. AT: "I still obey your instructions" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_litotes]])
