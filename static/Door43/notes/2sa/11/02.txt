===== translationNotes: =====

  * **So it came about** - "So it happened" or "So it came to pass." The author uses this phrase to introduce the next event in the storyline. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_newevent]])
  * **a woman who was bathing** - The woman was not on the roof, she would have been bathing outside in the courtyard of her house. AT: "a woman who was bathing in the courtyard of her house" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **So David sent** - Here the word "sent" means that David sent a messenger. AT: "So David sent a messenger" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **he asked people who would know about the woman** - David was trying to find who the woman who. The word "he" refers to David, but is a metonym for the messenger that David sent. The messenger was to ask the people for information about her. AT: "the messenger asked the people who knew her about who she was" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **Is not this Bathsheba ... and is she not the wife of Uriah the Hittite** - This question gives information and can be written as a statement. AT: "This is Bathsheba ... and she is the wife of Uriah the Hittite." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
