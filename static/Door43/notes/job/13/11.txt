===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Would not his majesty make you afraid? Would not his dread fall upon you?** - Job uses these questions to rebuke his friends. Possible meanings are 1) Job is saying that they should fear God. AT: "His majesty should make you afraid, and his dread should fall on you" or 2) Job is saying that they will fear God. AT: "His majesty will make you afraid, and his dread will fall on you!" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **Would not his dread fall upon you** - Dead falling on people represents them becoming terribly afraid. AT: "Would you not be terribly afraid" or "Would you not be terrified" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Your memorable sayings are proverbs made of ashes** - Ashes represent things that are worthless and do not last. AT: "Your memorable sayings are worthless like ashes" or "Your memorable sayings will be forgotten like ashes that are blown away" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **your defenses are defenses made of clay** - Job speaks of what they say as if it were a wall around a city made of clay; it cannot defend the people because clay breaks easily. AT: "What you say in defense is as useless as a wall of clay" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **your defenses** - Possible meanings are that this refers to 1) what they say to defend themselves or 2) what they say to defend God.
