===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Connecting Statement:** - Another angel begins to speak about Babylon. This is a different angel than the ones who have spoken previously.

  * **millstone** - a large round stone used to crush grain
  * **Babylon, the great city, will be thrown down with violence and will not be seen anymore** - God will completely destroy the city. This can be stated in active form. AT: "God will violently throw down Babylon, the great city, and it will no longer exist" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **and will not be seen anymore** - "and no one will see it anymore." Not being seen here means that it will not exist. AT: "it will not exist anymore" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **The sound of harpers, musicians, flute players, and trumpeters will not be heard anymore in you** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "No one will ever again hear the sound of harpers, musicians, flute players, and trumpeters in your city" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **in you** - The angel speaks as if Babylon were there listening to him. AT: "in Babylon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_apostrophe]])
  * **will not be heard anymore in you** - "no one will hear them anymore in you." Not being heard here means that they will not be there. AT: "they will not be in your city anymore" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **No craftsman ... will be found in you** - Not being found there means that they will not be there. AT: "No craftsman of any kind will be in your city" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **No sound of a mill will be heard anymore in you** - The sound of something not being heard means that no one will make that sound. AT: "No one will use the mills in your city" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
