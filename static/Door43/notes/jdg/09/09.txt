===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Jotham continues telling his parable, where trees represent various people groups.

  * **the olive tree said to them ... fig tree said to them** - In this parable, Jotham describes the trees as doing things that humans do. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_parables]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification|Personification]])
  * **Should I give up my oil ... over the other trees?** - The olive tree is asking this question to refuse to be king. This question can be expressed as a statement. AT: "I will not give up my oil ... over the other trees." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion|Rhetorical Question]])
  * **sway over** - To "sway over" means to move back and forth in the wind. Here the tree uses this phrase to means to "rule over."
  * **Should I give up my sweetness ... over the other trees?** - The fig tree is asking this question to refuse to be king. This question can be expressed as a statement. AT: "I will not give up my sweetness ... over the other trees." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion|Rhetorical Question]])
  * **my sweetness and my good fruit** - The word "sweetness" is an abstract noun. It can be stated as an adjective that describes the fruit that grows on the tree. AT: "my good sweet fruit" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns|Abstract Nouns]])
