===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Parallelism is common in Hebrew poetry. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry|Poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism|Parallelism]])

  * **he is robed in majesty; Yahweh has clothed and girded himself with strength** - The psalmist speaks of Yahweh's strength and majesty as if they were things Yahweh wears. AT: "he shows everyone that he is a powerful king" or "his majesty is there for all to see, like the robe a king wears; everything about Yahweh shows that he is strong and he is ready to do great work" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor|Metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile|Simile]])
  * **majesty** - the power of a king and the way a king acts
  * **girded himself** - put on a belt—a band of leather or another material that a person wears around his waist—to prepare for work or battle
  * **The world is firmly established** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "You have firmly established the world" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive|Active or Passive]])
  * **it cannot be moved** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "no one will ever move it" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive|Active or Passive]])
  * **Your throne is established from ancient times** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "You established your throne in ancient times" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive|Active or Passive]])
  * **you are from everlasting** - "you have always existed"
