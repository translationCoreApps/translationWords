===== translationNotes: =====

  * **the word of Yahweh came** - This is an idiom that is used to introduce something that God told his prophets or his people. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ezk:03:16|3:16]]. AT: "Yahweh spoke this message" or "Yahweh spoke these words" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **son of man** - "son of a human being" or "son of humanity." God calls Ezekiel this to emphasize that Ezekiel is only a human being. God is eternal and powerful, but humans are not. AT: "mortal person" or "human"
  * **begin a lamentation** - The abstract noun "lamentation" can be translated using the verb "lament." AT: "begin to lament" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **say to Tyre** - Here the word "Tyre" represents the people living in Tyre. AT: "say to the people of Tyre" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **who lives within the gates of the sea** - "who lives at the gates of the sea" or "who lives at the entry to the sea" 
  * **Tyre, you have said** - Here the word "Tyre" represents the people living in Tyre. AT: "People of Tyre, you have said" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **I am perfect in beauty** - The abstract noun "beauty" can be translated using the adjective "beautiful." AT: "I am perfectly beautiful" or "I am entirely beautiful" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
