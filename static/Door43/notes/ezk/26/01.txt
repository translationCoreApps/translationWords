===== translationNotes: =====

  * **in the eleventh year** - AT: "in the eleventh year of the exile of King Jehoiachin" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_ordinal]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **on the first day of the month** - It is uncertain which month of the Hebrew calendar Ezekiel meant. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_ordinal]])
  * **the word of Yahweh came** - "Yahweh spoke his word." See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ezk:01:01|1:1]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Son of man** - See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ezk:02:01|2:3]].
  * **Tyre has said against Jerusalem** - AT: "the people of Tyre have said against the people of Jerusalem." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Aha!** - AT: "Yes!" or "Very good!"  
  * **The gates of the people are broken** - The people of Tyre use these words to refer to Jerusalem as if it were a city gate through which traders from the surrounding nations pass. This can be stated in active form. AT: "Armies have broken down the gates of the people" or "Armies have destroyed Jerusalem, which was like gates through which people from many nations passed" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **She has turned to me** - Here the word "she" refers to the word "gates" which have "turned" on their hinges to open to Tyre. This phrase means that Tyre has now replaced Jerusalem as the city through which the traders from many nations pass. AT: "The gateway has opened to me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **I will be filled up** - Here to "be filled up" means to be prosperous. AT: "I will be prosperous" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **she is ruined** - "Jerusalem is ruined"
