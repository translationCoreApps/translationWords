===== translationNotes: =====

  * **The violence of the wicked will drag them away** - The writer speaks as though violence were a person who could drag other people away. God will punish wicked people who harm their innocent neighbors because of the violent deeds they have done. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **violence of the wicked** - The abstract noun "violence" refers to violent deeds or things people do to harm their innocent neighbors. The word "wicked" is a nominal adjective that can be translated as a noun phrase, "wicked people." AT: "The violent acts wicked people do to harm their innocent neighbors" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_nominaladj]])
  * **drag them away** - "take hold of them and force them to appear before God so that God can judge them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **The way of a guilty person is crooked** - This compares the way one lives to a crooked road one may travel. This is also an idiom. AT: "The way a guilty person lives is crooked" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **crooked** - Here "crooked" means bent or not straight. This is a metaphor for morally wrong. AT: "wrong" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
