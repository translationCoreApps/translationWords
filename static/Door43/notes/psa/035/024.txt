===== translationNotes: =====


  * **do not let them rejoice over me** - "do not let them be glad because I am suffering"
  * **say in their heart** - This is an idiom that means to say to oneself. AT: "say to themselves" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Aha** - This is an exclamation that emphasizes what follows. AT: "Yes! we have destroyed him, just like we wanted" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_exclamations]])
  * **we have what we wanted** - It is implied that the writer's enemies wanted him to be declared guilty. AT: "he has been declared guilty just as we desired!" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **We have devoured him** - "We have swallowed him up." The writer's enemies speak of his destruction as if they were wild animals who had eaten him. AT: "We have destroyed him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **confound** - "humiliate" or "perplex"
  * **May those who taunt me be covered with shame and dishonor** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "May you cover those who taunt me with shame and dishonor" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]]) 
  * **taunt** - insulting someone to make him angry
  * **covered with shame and dishonor** - These abstract nouns can be stated as verbs. Here shame and dishonor are spoken of as if they were shameful clothes that the writer could wear. AT: "shamed and dishonored" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **shame and dishonor** - These terms mean about the same thing and are used to emphasize who degraded they will be. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]])
