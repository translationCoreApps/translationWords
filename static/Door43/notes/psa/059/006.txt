===== translationNotes: =====

  * **They return at evening** - The word "they" refers to the wicked transgressors.
  * **they howl like dogs** - The psalmist speaks of his enemies threatening to attack people as if they were dogs howling, growling, or barking at people. AT: "they threaten to attack people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **go around the city** - Why they go around the city can be stated clearly. AT: "go around the city to attack anyone they find" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **See** - Here the word "See" is being used to draw attention to something. AT: "Hear"
  * **they belch out with their mouths** - Saying terrible things is spoken of as belching. These terrible things may have been insults or threats. AT: "they say terrible things" or "they shout out terrible words" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **belch** - burp loudly; allow air from the stomach to come out of the mouth with a loud, rude noise
  * **swords are in their lips** - The cruel things that the wicked people were saying are spoken of as if they were swords. AT: "they say cruel things that cause people trouble as much as swords destroy people"  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Who hears us?** - This question is used to show that they believed that God would not hear and punish them. AT: "No one can hear us!" or "Your God does not hear us!" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
