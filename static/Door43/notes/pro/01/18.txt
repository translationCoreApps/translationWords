===== translationNotes: =====


  * **These men lie in wait to kill themselves—they set a trap for themselves** - This finishes the comparison started in [[:en:bible:notes:pro:01:15|1:17]]. The men destroying themselves by doing sinful things is spoken of as if they set a trap and kill themselves. AT: "But these men are more foolish than the birds. They kill themselves with their own traps" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **So are the ways of everyone** - A person's fate or destiny is spoken of as if it were a road a person walks on. AT: "This is what happens to everyone" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **unjust gain takes away the lives of those who hold on to it** - A person destroying themselves by trying to gain wealth through violence, theft, and deceit is spoken of as if the unjust gains will kill those who take it. AT: "it is like the unjust gains will destroy those who hold on to it" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
