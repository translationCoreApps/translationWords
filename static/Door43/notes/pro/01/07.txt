===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - A father teaches his child.

  * **The fear of Yahweh is the beginning of knowledge** - The abstract nouns "fear," "beginning," and "knowledge" can be stated as verbs. AT: "You must first fear Yahweh in order to begin to know what is wise" or "You must first honor and respect Yahweh in order to learn what is wise" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **Yahweh** - This is the name of God that he revealed to his people in the Old Testament. See the translationWord page about Yahweh concerning how to translate this.
  * **fools despise wisdom and instruction** - "those who do not value what is wise and instructive are fools"
  * **do not lay aside** - This is an idiom that means "do not ignore" or "do not reject" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **they will be a graceful wreath for your head and pendants hanging from your neck** - The rules and instructions that parents teach their children are so valuable and important that they are spoken of as if they were a beautiful wreath or pendant that a person wears. AT: "they will make you wise just as wearing a wreath on your head or a pendant around your neck makes you beautiful" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **wreath** - an woven circle made of leaves or flowers
  * **pendants** - jewelry that is worn around the neck
