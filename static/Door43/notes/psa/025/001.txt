===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]

  * **A psalm of David** - Possible meanings are 1) David wrote the psalm or 2) the psalm is about David or 3) the psalm is in the style of David's psalms.
  * **I lift up my life** - The phrase "lift up my life" is a metaphor. Possible meanings are 1) the writer is giving himself to Yahweh, which means he is completely depending on Yahweh or 2) he is offering prayer and adoration to Yahweh. AT: 1) "I give my life to you" (UDB) or 2) "I worship and adore you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Do not let me be humiliated** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "Do not let my enemies humiliate me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **humiliated** - "ashamed"
  * **rejoice triumphantly over me** - "rejoice in triumph over me." The phrase "over me" implies that his enemies have defeated him and stand above in victory. AT: "defeat me and rejoice about it" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **May no one who hopes in you be disgraced** - "Do not let those who hope in you be disgraced." Disgrace could come from being defeated by their enemies. This can be stated in active form. AT: "Do not let enemies defeat those who hope in you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **who hopes in you** - "who trusts you"
  * **act treacherously** - "act deceitfully" or "act with trickery"
  * **without cause** - "without a reason"
