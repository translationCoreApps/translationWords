===== translationNotes: =====

  * **with all their heart and with all their soul** - The idiom "with all ... heart" means "completely" and "with all ... soul" means "with all ... being." These two phrases have similar meanings. Translate as in [[:en:bible:notes:1ki:02:01|2:4]]. AT: "with all their being" or "with all their energy" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]])
  * **in the land** - "while they are living in the land"
  * **toward their land** - "toward the land in which they belong." This refers to Israel.
  * **for your name** - The word "name" is a metonym for the person, and "for ... name" refers to worshiping the person. Similar words appear in [[:en:bible:notes:1ki:03:01|3:2]]. AT: "in which people will worship you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
