===== translationNotes: =====

  * **my words are life** - The writer speaks of his words preserving a person's life as if they were that person's life. AT: "my words give life" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **to those who find them** - The writer speaks of fully understanding his words as if the person searches for them and finds them. AT: "to those who understand and practice them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **health to their whole body** - The word "their" refers to "those who find them." The subject for this phrase can be supplied from the previous phrase. AT: "my words will give health to the whole body of those who find them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **Keep your heart safe and guard it** - Here the word "heart" represents a person's mind and thoughts. AT: "Keep your mind safe and guard your thoughts" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **with all diligence** - with constant and earnest effort
  * **from it flow the springs of life** - The word "it" refers to the heart, which is a metonym for the mind and thoughts. The writer speaks of a person's life as if it were a flowing spring that originates from the heart. AT: "from your thoughts comes everything you say and do" or "your thoughts determine your course of life" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
