===== translationNotes: =====

  * **So I will still accuse you** - Yahweh is speaking to the people of Israel.
  * **this is Yahweh's declaration** - Yahweh speaks of himself by name to express the certainty of what he is declaring. Translate as in [[:en:bible:notes:jer:01:07|1:8]]. AT: "this is what Yahweh has declared" or "this is what I, Yahweh, have declared" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **your sons' sons** - "your future generations"
  * **cross over to the coasts of Kittim** - Kittim was an island west of Israel. Today it is called Cyprus. It represented all the lands far to the west of Israel. AT: "Go west across the ocean to Kittim" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Send messengers out to Kedar** - Kedar is the name of a land far to the east of Israel. AT: "Send messengers far east to the land of Kedar" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Has a nation exchanged gods ... gods?** - God uses this question to tell the people of Israel that other nations continue to worship their own gods. They do not change and worship other gods. AT: "You will see that no nation has ever exchanged ... gods." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **But my people have exchanged their glory for what cannot help them** - Here God speaks as though he were not speaking directly to his people. AT: "But you, my people, have exchanged me, your glorious God, for what cannot help you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **their glory** - This expression refers to God himself, who is glorious. AT: "their glorious God" or "me, your glorious God" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **for what cannot help** - Here false gods are spoken of in terms of what they cannot do. AT: "for false gods, which cannot help" or "gods who cannot help" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
