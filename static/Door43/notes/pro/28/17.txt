===== translationNotes: =====

  * **he has shed someone's blood** - Here "blood" represents a person's life. To "shed blood" means to murder someone. AT: "he has killed someone" or "he has murdered someone" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **fugitive** - a person who is running away to avoid being captured
  * **until death** - "until he dies." This means for the rest of his life. AT: "all of his life" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Whoever walks with integrity will be kept safe** - This can be expressed in active form. AT: "God will keep safe anyone who walks with integrity" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **Whoever** - "Anyone who"
  * **walks with integrity** - This represents a person living a life of integrity. The abstract noun "integrity" can be expressed as an adverb. AT: "walks honestly" or "lives honestly" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **the one whose way is crooked** - A dishonest person is spoken of as if he walks on crooked or twisted paths. AT: "the one who does not live honestly" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **will suddenly fall** - What will happen to a dishonest person is spoken of as if he suddenly fell down. AT: "will suddenly be ruined" or "will suddenly perish" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
