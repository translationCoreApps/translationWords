===== translationNotes: =====


  * **I hold fast to my righteousness** - Here "hold fast" to is a metaphor that represents being determined to continue to say something. The abstract noun "righteousness" can be expressed with "righteous." AT: "I am determined to continue saying that I am righteous" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **will not let it go** - Here "will not let it go" is a metaphor that represents stopping saying something. AT: "will not stop saying that I am righteous" or "will not stop saying so" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **my thoughts will not reproach me** - Here "my thoughts" represents Job. AT: "even in my thoughts, I will not reproach myself" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Let my enemy be ... let him who rises up against me be** - The two clauses that start with these words share the same meaning. They are used together to emphasize Job's strong desire that this should happen. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]]) 
  * **Let my enemy be like a wicked man** - How he wants his enemy to be like a wicked person can be stated clearly. AT: "Let my enemy be punished like a wicked man" or "Let God punish my enemy as he punishes wicked people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **let him who rises up against me be like an unrighteous man** - How he wants this person to be like an unrighteous man person can be stated clearly. AT: "Let him who rises up against me be punished like an unrighteous man" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **him who rises up against me** - Here "rises up against me" is a metaphor meaning oppose him. The whole phrase refers to Job's adversary. AT: "him who opposes me" or "my adversary" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
