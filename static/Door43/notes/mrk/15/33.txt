===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Connecting Statement:** - At noon darkness covers the whole land until three o'clock when Jesus cries out with a loud voice and dies, and the temple curtain rips from the top to the bottom. 

  * **the sixth hour** - "Sixth" is a ordinal number. This refers to noon or 12 p.m. AT: "noon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_ordinal]])
  * **darkness came over the whole land** - Here the author describes it becoming dark outside as if the darkness were a wave that moved over the land. AT: "the whole land became dark" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the ninth hour** - "Ninth" is a ordinal number. This refers to three o'clock in the afternoon. AT: "three o'clock in the afternoon" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_ordinal]])
  * **Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani** - These are Aramaic words that should be copied as is into your language with similar sounds. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_transliterate]])
  * **is interpreted** - "means" 
  * **Some of those standing by heard it** - The word "Eloi" is an Aramaic word that sounds very similar to the Aramaic word for Elijah. The people misunderstood what Jesus had said. AT: "When some of the people who were standing there heard the word 'Eloi,' they misunderstood it" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
