===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** Job continues speaking to God.

  * **I would answer** - "I would respond"
  * **You would have a desire for** - The noun "desire" can be expressed with the verbs "desire" or "want." AT: "You would desire" or "You would want" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **for the work of your hands** - Here God's hands represent him making things. Job refers to himself as the work of God's hands. AT: "for me, whom you have made" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **number and care for** - These two verbs together express a single action. AT: "attentively care for" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hendiadys]])
  * **my footsteps** - Footsteps represent his life or what he does  AT: "my life" or "the things I do" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **you would not keep ... My transgression would be ... you would cover up** - These three lines express the same thought and are used together to emphasize his confidence that God would forgive him. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **you would not keep track of my sin** - Keeping track of Job's sin represents thinking about his sin. AT: "you would not look at my sin" or "you would not think about my sin" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **My transgression would be sealed up in a bag** - Sealing transgression in a bag represents hiding it and refusing to think about it. This can be stated in active form. AT: "You would refuse to think about my transgression like someone who hides something in a bag" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **you would cover up my iniquity** - Covering up something iniquity so that it cannot be seen represents refusing to think about. AT: "you would hide my iniquity" or "you would ignore my iniquity" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
