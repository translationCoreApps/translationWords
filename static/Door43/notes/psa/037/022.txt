===== translationNotes: =====

  * **will inherit the land** - The possession of the land is spoken of it had been received as an inheritance. AT: "will receive the land as their own possession" or "will be allowed to live safely in the land." See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:037:008|37:9]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **those who are cursed by him will be cut off** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "those who Yahweh curses will be removed from the land" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **will be cut off** - The destruction of the wicked is spoken of as if they were a branch of plant that was cut off and thrown away. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:037:008|37:9]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **It is by Yahweh that a man's steps are established ... commendable in God's sight** - It might be helpful to reorder these phrases to show the logical connection. AT: "If a man lives in a commendable way in Yahweh's sight, Yahweh will establish his steps"
  * **It is by Yahweh that a man's steps are established** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "Yahweh is the one who enables a man to be successful" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **a man ... the man** - This does not refer to a specific man, but to men in general. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_genericnoun]])
  * **a man's steps** - Steps represents a person's conduct of his life. AT: "the way a man lives" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Though he stumbles, he will not fall down** - Here "stumble" and "fall" refer to the man's reaction to difficult times. AT: "Though he has difficult times, he will not utterly fail" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **holding him with his hand** - Yahweh is given the human attribute of having a hand. Here "his hand" refers to Yahweh's power, and "holding him" refers to protecting him. AT: "protecting him with his power" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
