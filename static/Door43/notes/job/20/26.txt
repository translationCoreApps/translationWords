===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Complete darkness is reserved for his treasures** - "Complete darkness" is a metaphor for destruction.   AT: "Destruction is reserved for his treasures" or "His treasures will be destroyed" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **a fire not fanned will devour him** - Here "devour" is a metaphor meaning destroy. AT: "a fire that is not fanned will destroy him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **a fire not fanned will devour him** - The phrase "not fanned" implies that no human will start the fire. Rather God will cause the fire. AT: "a fire that is not started by humans will destroy him" or "God will cause a fire to destroy him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **it will consume** - Here "consume" is a metaphor meaning destroy. AT: "the fire will destroy" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **The heavens ... the earth** - Possible meanings are: 1) those who live in the heavens and the earth (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]]), or 2) Zophar is describing the heavens and the earth as if they are humans who will testify in court against the wicked person. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
